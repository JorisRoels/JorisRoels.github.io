Research

The overall objective of my research is to develop 3D segmentation techniques and automate Electron Microscopy (EM) image processing as much as possible. Currently used algorithms require more ergonomics, higher computational performance and better segmentation quality, even with degraded input images. The goal is to compute a qualitative segmentation of 3D EM data sets where manual work of a single data set is limited to hours instead of months. This manual work is intended especially for training the parameters for a new application or dataset and solve a limited number of remaining problems by post-processing. This allows much more samples to be analyzed than before using the same equipment and the same manpower.

3D Electron Microscopy (EM)
3D EM is a state-of-the-art image acquisition technique aiming to capture ultrastructural cell content at high resolution. Within the context of my research, I am analyzing image artifacts, introduced by 3D EM and developing techniques to remove them, whenever they cannot be avoided at acquisition level.

Image Restoration
Noise and blur are significant artifacts in 3D EM. I am working on denoising and deconvolution algorithms in order to reduce their impact on subsequent image analysis. This is done within a probabilistic, regularized framework.
Image Segmentation
A crucial initial step in microscopy image analysis is segmentation. More particular, biological experts are currently researching mitochondria and endoplasmatic reticulum in order to fully understand e.g. Parkinson's disease, Alzheimer, etc. Because segmentation of these structures is very time-consuming and tedious work, I am developing automatic superpixel-based segmentation algorithms for these applications.
